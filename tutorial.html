Welcome to the Tutorial

Your guide to understanding on how to transfer a fresh leaf into herbarium specimens

The Tutorial

Step 1: Collecting

Tips for Collecting

  • Include both flowers and fruit if possible
  • For herbaceous plants, be sure that enough of the below-ground plants are available to show what sort of root system it has

Collecting specimens can be difficult and troublesome. Many different types of plants that can cause confusion at first glance. This section will help you to collect your desired specimen with some tips and tricks.

Here are the Steps for Specimen Collection:

  1. Obtain permissions from authorities
  2. Choose samples that look healthy
  3. Clean off the mud from the roots.
  4. Bend the stem or branch if it is too long.

Step 2: Describing and Record

When collecting, attach a label to the specimen recording. Here are the key informations to record:

Scientific name
The Latin binomial name of the plant species
Collection date
The exact date when the specimen was collected
Location data
Precise location including GPS coordinates if possible
Habitat description
Details about the environment where the plant was found

Step 3: Pressing

Use a press made with a pair of hardboard or plywood boards cut to the same size as the drying paper. Place some corrugated card on one board, then place two sheets of the blotting paper on top of this. Arrange your plant material on blotting paper retaining the character of the plant. Remove leaves and flowers of congested specimens to reduce the bulk without losing the character of the plant.

Pro Tips:

  • Make sure to use fresh drying paper to retain the best quality of your specimen.
  • Change the blotting drying frequently to avoid mold or decay during the drying process. This ensures your specimens maintain their natural color and shape.

Step 4: Mounting

The mounting stage is especially confusing if the steps aren't properly done. Using the proper materials is crucial for this step as many problems can arise later on if done wrongly. Here are the steps :

  • Prepare the mounting paper
  • Arrange the specimen
  • Attach the specimen using Neutral-pH PVA adhesive or Neutral-pH adhesive, gummed linen hanging tape
  • Add labels with important information
  • Allow the mounted specimen to dry completely

Step 5: Storage

The specimens kept in herbariums will be kept for many years and will be kept as future reference for people that needs it. This is why keeping the storage in optimal conditions are crucial for maintaining a good herbarium storage.

Remember to maintain your storage to keep any pests and things that would potentially damage the specimens out

Storage Maintanence

Precautions Uses
Flash freezing Kill any pests that may eat and destroy the specimens
Low light Extreme light can damage the specimens
Low humidity To prevent mold from growing on the specimens
