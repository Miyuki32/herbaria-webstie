Welcome to the tools

Your guide to understanding on the tools being used to create your own herbarium specimens.

What are the tools required?

Collecting

Collection Tools

Secateurs

Secateurs feature sharp blades that make clean cuts on stems, branches, and flowers, promoting healthy growth and preventing damage to plants. Available in various types—such as bypass, anvil, and ratchet—secateurs are chosen based on the specific tasks at hand. Regular maintenance, including sharpening blades and cleaning, is crucial for optimal performance.

Measuring tape

Measuring tape is an essential tool for gardeners and landscapers, used for accurately measuring distances, plant spacing, and garden bed dimensions. Typically made of flexible, durable materials, measuring tapes come in various lengths and widths, allowing for easy handling in tight spaces or over long distances. Accurate measurements are crucial for planning layouts, ensuring proper spacing for healthy plant growth, and achieving desired aesthetics.

Describing

Tools for describing

RHS Colour Chart

RHS Colour Charts are essential tools in herbarium studies, providing standardized color references that aid in the accurate identification and documentation of plant specimens. By recording the closest RHS color code alongside specimen details on labels, researchers can preserve important color information, even as specimens fade over time. This integration enhances the usability of herbarium collections, facilitating better communication and research in botany and plant conservation.

Pressing

Main tools

  • Two pieces of hardboard/plywood measuring approximately 40 x 30cm (16 x 12in)
  • Sheets of blotting paper

Additional Equipment

  • Sheets of corrugated card
  • Foam sheet
  • Bricks/telephone directories/weighty books/straps

Mounting

The tools required for mounting are as followed :

  • Acid-free paper measuring 42 x 26.5cm/ A3
  • Labels, preferably acid-free
  • Tweezers
  • Neutral-pH PVA adhesive
  • Neutral-pH adhesive, gummed linen hanging tape

Storage

Types of Storage

A good storage is crucial for a good herbarium as the specimens will be kept for many years. However, the storage types that are often used depends on personal preference and budget. Here are the most common storage types and their advantages :

Plastic bags

Advantages

Having the lowest cost, plastic bags can be used as temporary storage for the specimens.

Freezer

Advantages

Freezers are a sure fire way of keeping the specimens safe and secure. Specimens kept in freezers can be kept for a very long time as little damage can be done to the specimen.

Boxes

Advantages

Boxes can minimize the exposure of sunlight, water vapour and others to the specimens.
